Cross-Posting is Lazy Social Media Strategy

Sep 3, 2015

cross-posting (n): automatically posting content from one social network to one or more other social networks.

Cross-posting is lazy social media strategy.

Social media users demand content tailored to the audience. Even though the audience consists of most of the same people between social networks, those people expect different types of content on each social network.

Instagram:
- low-frequency posts
- long-form text content is common in the post description
- focus on the media, not the network

Facebook:
- multimedia posts
- enough text content to explain the media in the post
- emphasis on engagement in comments

Twitter:
- high-frequency posts
- brief text content
- multimedia posts get the same screen space as text-only posts
